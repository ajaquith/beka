Who We Serve

Nonprofit organizations

Organizations that tackle inequity, create opportunity and inspire creativity make up the fabric of cohesive, vibrant and just communities.

To survive and thrive in today’s fluid environment, mission-based organizations must build the knowledge, tools and capacities to support continuous adaptation. Ask yourself:

  • What resources are needed to respond to the evolving needs of your constituents and communities?
  • Are you ready to embark on a period of change?
  • Have you considered the business implications of your program plans?
  • Do your mission, strategy and financial story align in your case for support?

RTA offers a continuum of customized training and consulting services to help your organization adapt in ways that contribute to long-term health and impact.

Grantmakers

Navigating change requires new ways of connecting money to strategy, and strategy to mission. It demands that philanthropic leaders re-imagine how they structure and deploy investments and that nonprofit leaders re-conceive of how they raise and manage limited resources to increase their organizations’ effectiveness. Key questions:

  • Are your resources aligned to support the outcomes you seek?
  • Is your staff equipped to assess readiness for organizational change and risk-taking?
  • Are you effectively communicating the impact of your investments?

RTA brings strategic advice and hands-on support to help your foundation strengthen the alignment of money and mission.

The Field

RTA collaborates with nonprofits, foundations and intermediaries to create and implement the programs, processes and communications that influence and support new ways of operating and achieving impact.

We develop knowledge about the condition and needs of nonprofit organizations, and ideas for improving the strategies and structures that underpin community impact.

In the process, we create and elevate practical knowledge and ideas about what works – and what does not - at both the systems and organizational level.

Strategy Case Study

Thought partner to leading foundation

RTA serves as thought partner to a private foundation, helping program staff define and prioritize strategic goals and tactics that strengthen the local arts ecology and engender transformative cultural leadership. We will serve as a facilitator for internal and external conversations that explore critical issues in the areas of leadership development, creative communities, and capacity building. A result of this work will be the development of grantmaking approaches that advance the program’s priorities.

Knowledge Case Study

Capitalization trainings for funders

RTA facilitates Conversations on Capitalization, a daylong workshop for cultural funders that explores the financial health of local arts communities and the role philanthropists can play in building more stable and adaptable organizations. Offered by Grantmakers in the Arts (GIA), the workshop develops a common vocabulary about capitalization and the relationship between financial health and creative freedom. Each discussion is grounded in the realities of the local eco-system and reveals how funders can change their policies and practice to have more candid discussions about finances with their constituents.

Execution Case Studies

Investment program evaluation

In partnership with a leading nonprofit finance intermediary, RTA is conducting an evaluation of a national foundation’s capital investment program. Among the questions the evaluation seeks to answer are: Did grantees improve their financial health as a result of our investment? Did their business models become more reliable? How are they using knowledge about capitalization to improve management practices and fundraising outcomes? This project involves developing recommendations for how future, similar grant programs can strategically invest in capitalization as a means to mission success.

Grant management and opportunity assessment

RTA provides portfolio management services for the cultural program of a private foundation. We are conducting grantee due diligence and recommending strategies to align program and capitalization goals. As the foundation explores adding a new nonprofit cohort to its portfolio, we will also help assess these opportunities.
